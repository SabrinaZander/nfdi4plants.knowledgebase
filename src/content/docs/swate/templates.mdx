## Template selection

A good starting point for your data annotation is using a predefined template. It displays you all available templates of your selected communities. By clicking on the button "Filter Communities", on the right of the search input, you can select and deselct the communites you want to choose templates from. By default DataPlant communities are displayed. When you have selected your templates by clicking on them, you click on the "Import Templates" button, to select the import parameters of your templates.

## Template import

In the import modal of your templates you can select different parameter, that determine the way a template will be imported. The "Import Type" determines, whether you import a only the headers of your template, add units to the terms, or also import the values that are part of the template. The "MIMS" category enables you to decide for each template to be added to the currently active table, to be imported as a new table or not to be imported at all. Finally, you can customize the imported table further by clicking on the "Preview Selected Columns". Here, all columns of the table are represented and also their values. You can click on the columns that you do not want to import and click again on them to import them again.

## Additional Filter Parameters

You can filter the templates not only by their community but also by authors, tags, and specific templates. In order to do so, type the desired one into the search field and click on it. It will be added to the search input then and be an additional filter parameters. These search parameters can be removed by clicking on them in the search input. For an easier identification, they have different symbols.

## Template information

You can view additional information of templates without selecting them and viewing them in the import modal. For that, you can just click on the arrow down menu in the template search. There you will see additonal information, regadring its authors, current version and associated organisation. The display of the additional information can easily be removed by clicking on the x button on its right side.
